Case Name: Vijay Kumar Ghai v. State of West Bengal
Citation: (2022) 7 SCC 124
Date: 2022
Court: Supreme Court of India

Summary:
The Supreme Court quashed criminal proceedings arising from a commercial dispute. The case involved a contract for the supply of goods where the complainant alleged criminal breach of trust (Section 406) and cheating (Section 420) after a dispute over delivery and payments.

Key Ruling:
1. Every breach of contract does not give rise to a criminal offence of breach of trust or cheating.
2. For Section 406 IPC (Criminal Breach of Trust), there must be a specific entrustment of property and a subsequent dishonest misappropriation.
3. For Section 420 IPC (Cheating), the fraudulent or dishonest intention must exist at the inception of the transaction.
4. The Court warned against the trend of criminalizing civil and commercial disputes.

Legal Principles:
- Distinction between civil breach of contract and criminal breach of trust.
- Requirement of mens rea in commercial transactions.
- Quashing of FIR in purely civil matters.
