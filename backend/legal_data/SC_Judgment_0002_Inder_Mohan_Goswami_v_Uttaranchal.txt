Case Name: Inder Mohan Goswami v. State of Uttaranchal
Citation: (2007) 12 SCC 1
Date: 2007
Court: Supreme Court of India

Summary:
The case involved a dispute over a land sale agreement where the vendors were accused of cheating and forgery after they cancelled the agreement and entered into a transaction with a third party.

Key Ruling:
1. To attract Section 420 IPC (Cheating), it must be shown that the accused had a dishonest intention at the time of making the promise or representation.
2. Subsequent failure to fulfill the promise does not necessarily constitute cheating.
3. Criminal proceedings should not be used as a shortcut for resolving civil disputes.
4. The High Court has the power under Section 482 CrPC to quash proceedings to prevent abuse of the process of any court.

Legal Principles:
- Intention at the inception is the gist of the offence of cheating.
- Abuse of process of law in commercial/property disputes.
