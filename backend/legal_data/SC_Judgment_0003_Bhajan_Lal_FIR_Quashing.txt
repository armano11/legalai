Case Name: State of Haryana v. Ch. Bhajan Lal
Citation: 1992 Supp (1) SCC 335
Date: 1992
Court: Supreme Court of India

Summary:
This landmark judgment laid down the guidelines for the exercise of extraordinary powers by the High Court under Article 226 of the Constitution or Section 482 of the CrPC for quashing an FIR or a criminal complaint.

Key Principles for Quashing:
1. Where the allegations made in the FIR, even if taken at face value, do not prima facie constitute any offence.
2. Where the allegations are so absurd and inherently improbable that no prudent person can ever reach a just conclusion that there is sufficient ground for proceeding.
3. Where there is an express legal bar engrafted in any of the provisions of the Code or the concerned Act.
4. Where a criminal proceeding is manifestly attended with mala fide and/or where the proceeding is maliciously instituted with an ulterior motive for wreaking vengeance on the accused.

Relevance to Commercial Fraud:
Often cited in partnership and business disputes where one partner files a criminal case against another to settle civil dues or exert pressure.
