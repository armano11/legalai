Case Name: RK Dalmia v. Delhi Administration
Citation: AIR 1962 SC 1821
Date: 1962
Court: Supreme Court of India

Summary:
A landmark case regarding Criminal Breach of Trust (Section 405/409 IPC) by an agent/director. The case involved the diversion of funds from an insurance company to other businesses controlled by the accused.

Key Ruling:
1. "Property" in Section 405 includes chose-in-action and money.
2. Entrustment can occur in any manner and does not require the creation of a formal trust.
3. Dishonest misappropriation occurs when the accused uses the entrusted property for a purpose other than that for which it was entrusted, in violation of the owner's rights.
4. Directors and partners can be held liable for criminal breach of trust if they dishonestly misappropriate firm assets for personal use.

Legal Principles:
- Definition of entrustment and dominion over property.
- Misappropriation of funds by directors/partners.
- Criminal liability in corporate/business management.
